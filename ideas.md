# Design Brainstorming: Tech Hilfe Pro - B2C Section

<idea>
  **Design Movement**: "Neumorphic Clean Tech"
  
  **Core Principles**:
  1. **Soft Approachability**: Technology can be intimidating; the design should feel soft, safe, and welcoming.
  2. **Clarity First**: Information density is high (17 services, 3 plans), so layout must prioritize readability and scanning.
  3. **Trust through Polish**: High-end execution builds trust in technical competence.
  
  **Color Philosophy**:
  - **Primary**: Deep Trust Blue (`oklch(0.45 0.15 260)`) - Professionalism and reliability.
  - **Secondary**: Warm Signal Orange (`oklch(0.65 0.18 45)`) - For CTAs and highlights, adding warmth.
  - **Background**: Very light cool grey/blue (`oklch(0.98 0.01 250)`) - Clinical but not sterile.
  - **Surface**: Pure white with subtle neumorphic shadows for cards.
  
  **Layout Paradigm**:
  - **Asymmetric Hero**: Left-aligned text with a "floating" 3D illustration on the right that breaks the container bounds.
  - **Masonry-ish Grid**: For service cards, using a responsive grid that feels organic rather than rigid.
  - **Floating Pricing**: Pricing cards that appear to hover above the background with significant depth.
  
  **Signature Elements**:
  - **Soft Shadows**: Multi-layered shadows to create depth without harsh borders.
  - **Rounded Corners**: Generous border-radius (1rem+) to reinforce friendliness.
  - **Glassmorphism**: Subtle frosted glass effects on sticky headers or overlays.
  
  **Interaction Philosophy**:
  - **Hover Lift**: Cards gently lift and shadow deepens on hover.
  - **Smooth Toggles**: The monthly/yearly toggle should feel like a physical switch with weight.
  - **Micro-interactions**: Icons that subtly animate on hover.
  
  **Animation**:
  - **Staggered Entrance**: Service cards fade in one by one.
  - **Smooth Expand**: Accordions for FAQs or details open with spring physics.
  
  **Typography System**:
  - **Headings**: "Outfit" or "Plus Jakarta Sans" - Modern, geometric but friendly.
  - **Body**: "Inter" or "DM Sans" - Highly legible for dense text.
</idea>
</text>
